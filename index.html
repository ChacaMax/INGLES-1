Módulo 3 — Work-life & People

Daily routines, actions happening now, quantities, frequencies and abilities.

5.1 Present Continuous

Usamos el Presente Continuo para hablar de acciones que están ocurriendo en este momento o en este período de tiempo (acciones temporales).

VERB TO BE + ING FORM
PersonaAfirmativaNegativaInterrogativa
Iam workingI'm not workingAm I working?
He / She / Itis workingisn't workingIs he working?
You / They / Weare workingaren't workingAre they working?
⚠️ Verbos que NO usan Present Continuous: know · like · want · hate · love · belong · mean · understand · remember · prefer
Reglas ortográficas para -ING:
  • En general: añadir -ing (listen → listening)
  • Verbo termina en consonante + e: se elimina la e (write → writing)
  • Verbo termina en -ie: cambia a -y (die → dying)
  • Monosílabos con vocal corta + consonante: duplicar consonante final (stop → stopping)
At this moment, many young people are looking for jobs.
En este momento, muchos jóvenes están buscando trabajo.
Now, we are planning next year's activities.
Ahora, estamos planeando las actividades del año próximo.

Preguntas

WH questions → info What are you doing? → I am speaking.
Yes/No questions Are you studying? → Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
💡 Usos del Present Continuous:
  1. Acción ocurriendo ahora mismo — "She's watering the plants now"
  2. Acción temporal (no en este instante) — "My brother is working in a pizza restaurant"
  3. Planes futuros confirmados — "He's having dinner with us tonight"
  4. Habitual + irritación (con always) — "He's always talking about himself"
5.2 Quantifiers

Los cuantificadores expresan cantidad: how much (incontables) / how many (contables).

SOME → oraciones afirmativas We have some problems.
He's got some work to do.
ANY → oraciones negativas / preguntas We don't have any problems.
Do you have any idea?
NO + noun = NOT A / NOT ANY There is no bus. (= there isn't a bus)
NONE → sin sustantivo "How much money?" → "None."

A little vs A few:
a little = pequeña cantidad (incontable): We have a little time.
a few = pequeño número (contable): I have a few friends.
6.1 Adverbs of Frequency
Always — Siempre
Almost always — Casi siempre
Usually — Usualmente
Often — A menudo
Sometimes — A veces
Hardly ever — Casi nunca
Almost never — Casi nunca
Never — Nunca
📍 Posición del adverbio:
ANTES del verbo principal: I hardly ever watch TV.
DESPUÉS del verbo To Be: I am always at home.
*Sometimes puede ir al inicio de la oración.
How often do you exercise? → I go jogging once a week.
Do you ever watch TV? → I sometimes watch TV before bed.
I don't exercise very often. / I swim about three times a year.
6.2 Questions with HOW
PreguntaTipo de respuestaEjemplo

How often do you go on vacations? → Once a year.
How good are you at swimming? → Not so good. I'm learning.

Módulo 4 — Past Events & Places

Speaking about the past and describing locations & directions.

7.1 Past Simple

Usamos el Past Simple para hablar de acciones completadas en un tiempo definido del pasado.

Marcadores de tiempo del Past Simple: yesterday last week/month/year ago in 2015

Verbos Regulares → agregar -ED

PersonaAfirmativaNegativa (DID NOT)Ejemplo
IWORKEDdidn't workI worked for a company.
YouVISITEDdidn't visitYou visited your grandmother.
He/She/ItTRAVELEDdidn't travelShe traveled to Spain.
They / WePLAYEDdidn't playWe played tennis as kids.
DID + NOT + BASE FORM OF THE VERB (infinitive without "to")

Preguntas en Past Simple

WH question What did you do? → I played in the street.
Yes/No question Did you play? → Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.

Verbo TO BE en pasado

PronombresAfirmativoNegativo
I / He / She / Itwaswas not / wasn't
You / They / Wewerewere not / weren't
She was in Europe last year. / They were not at the office.
Was Peter at the office yesterday? → Yes, he was.
The chairman decided to sell the company last month.
El presidente decidió vender la empresa el mes pasado.
8.1 Present Perfect

Usamos el Present Perfect para hablar de acciones que empezaron en el pasado pero tienen relación con el presente (tiempo indefinido).

HAS / HAVE + PAST PARTICIPLE
PersonaVerbo HAVEPast ParticipleEjemplo
SHE / HE / ITHASworked / written / beenShe has worked hard.
I / YOU / THEY / WEHAVEdone / had / goneI have done many things.
Past Participle = 3ª columna del verbo:
go → went → gone  |  win → won → won  |  see → saw → seen
(Verbos regulares: 2ª y 3ª columna son iguales)

Usos y marcadores de tiempo

#UsoMarcadoresEjemplo
1Acción que empezó en el pasado y continúaSINCE / FORI have lived here since 2011.
2Experiencia hasta ahoraEVER / NEVER / YET / SO FAR / UP TO NOWHave you ever been to Paris?
3Pasado con relación al presenteI can't enter. I have lost my keys.
4Pasado recienteJUST / RECENTLY / LATELYShe has just finished her homework.
5Antes de lo esperadoALREADYI have already finished.
6Acción repetidaTWICE / THREE TIMES / SEVERAL TIMESShe has gone to Boston several times.
🚫 NUNCA usar Present Perfect con: yesterday last week ago → Usar Simple Past en su lugar.
✅ She went to the movies yesterday.
❌ She has gone to the movies yesterday. — WRONG

Negativa e Interrogativa

HAVEN'T / HASN'T + Past Participle | HAVE/HAS + Subject + Past Participle?
8.2 There is / There are

There is / There are = hay en español.

Tipo de sustantivoAfirmativoNegativoInterrogativo
Singular / incontableThere is a bank.There isn't a bank.Is there a bank?
Plural / contableThere are some parks.There aren't any parks.Are there any parks?
Is there a bank near here? → Yes, there is. There's one across from the fast food restaurant.
Are there any parks around here? → Yes, there are. There are some nice parks on Libertador Street.
8.3 Prepositions of Place

Las prepositions of place indican dónde están las cosas, personas o lugares.

In front of

On the corner of

SINCE vs FOR
SINCE = desde un punto en el tiempo
I have lived here since 2011.
She has worked here since January.
FOR = durante un período de tiempo
She has studied English for 10 years.
I have been here for two hours.
